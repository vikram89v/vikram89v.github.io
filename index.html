About

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Materials Modelling Laboratory at the University of Oxford, working with Prof. Saiful Islam as part of the EPSRC UKRI Programme Grant ‘Advanced Device Concepts for Next-Generation Photovoltaics’, in collaboration with Prof. Henry Snaith. My research combines density functional theory, atomistic simulations, and machine-learned interatomic potentials to investigate electronic structure, defect chemistry, surface and interface phenomena, and coupled charge, ion, and heat transport in functional materials.

My work has helped resolve major stability challenges in halide perovskite photovoltaics — from bandgap-universal defect passivation (Science, 2024) to vertically oriented low-dimensional perovskites (Nature Communications, 2024) — and extends to thermoelectrics, catalysis, and energy materials in general. Before Oxford, I worked on low-cost thermoelectric materials at the University of Reading, following an MSc-PhD in Physics at IIT Bombay.

Research Interests

Atomistic insight into functional materials for sustainable energy technologies.

Photovoltaics & Optoelectronics

Halide perovskites for solar cells and LEDs: passivation, stability, halide segregation, and interface chemistry.

Defects, Interfaces & Ion Transport

How defect chemistry, grain boundaries, and interface structure govern ion migration, degradation, and device stability.

Thermoelectrics & Thermal Transport

Phonon transport, lattice thermal conductivity, and design of low-cost thermoelectric materials from earth-abundant elements.

Catalysis & Photocatalysis

Electrocatalytic CO₂ reduction, MXene surfaces, and photocatalytic materials from first principles.

Materials Discovery & Design

High-throughput screening and data-driven design of new energy and quantum materials, guided by first-principles accuracy.

Broader Energy Materials

Extending defect, disorder, and transport frameworks to batteries, hydrogen storage, ion conductors, and critical-mineral materials.
